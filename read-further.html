Uzbek Plov Recipe

Ingredients

  • 2 cups basmati or long-grain rice
  • 1 lb (450g) lamb or beef, cut into bite-sized pieces1 lb (450g) lamb or beef, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 2 large carrots, julienned
  • 2 large onions, thinly sliced
  • 4 cloves garlic, peeled
  • 1/2 cup vegetable oil or lamb fat
  • 1 tablespoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon coriander seeds
  • 1 teaspoon paprika

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Rice:
    • Rinse the rice under cold water until the water runs clear. Soak the rice in cold water for at least 30 minutes, then drain.
  2. Cook the Meat and Vegetables:
    • In a large, heavy-bottomed pot or a traditional Uzbek kazan, heat the vegetable oil or lamb fat over medium-high heat.
    • Add the onions and cook until they are golden brown.
    • Add the meat and cook until browned on all sides.
    • Stir in the carrots and cook for another 5 minutes until they start to soften.
  3. Add Spices and Flavor:
    • Add the cumin seeds, coriander seeds, paprika, black pepper, and salt to the pot. Stir well to coat the meat and vegetables with the spices.
    • Add the garlic cloves, raisins, and chickpeas (if using), and mix them into the pot.
  4. Layer the Rice:
    • Spread the soaked and drained rice evenly over the meat and vegetables in the pot. Do not stir.
    • Pour the water gently over the rice to cover it by about 1 inch (2.5 cm).
  5. Simmer and Cook:
    • Bring the water to a boil, then reduce the heat to low. Cover the pot tightly with a lid and let it simmer for about 25-30 minutes, or until the rice is cooked and the water is absorbed.
    • Do not stir the rice while it is cooking.
  6. Steam and Serve:
    • Once the rice is cooked, remove the pot from heat. Place a clean kitchen towel under the lid and let the Plov steam for another 10 minutes.
    • Gently fluff the rice with a fork, mixing the layers of meat and vegetables with the rice.
  7. Presentation:
    • Transfer the Plov to a large serving dish, making sure to showcase the tender meat and vibrant carrots on top.
    • Serve hot, garnished with additional cumin seeds or fresh herbs if desired.
