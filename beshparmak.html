Central Asian Infamous Beshparmak

Beshparmak, meaning "five fingers," is a traditional Central Asian dish typically made with boiled meat and served over flat noodles. It's a hearty and flavorful meal enjoyed by many.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs lamb or beef, cut into large chunks
  • 2 onions, thinly sliced
  • 2 carrots, sliced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 bay leaves
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 lb flat noodles (or pasta)
  • Fresh dill for garnish

Instructions

  1. In a large pot, add the meat chunks and cover with water. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for about 2 hours, or until the meat is tender.
  2. Remove the meat from the pot and set aside. Skim off any impurities from the broth.
  3. Add the sliced onions, carrots, garlic, and bay leaves to the pot. Simmer for another 20-30 minutes, or until the vegetables are tender.
  4. Meanwhile, cook the flat noodles according to package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  5. Return the meat to the pot to warm through. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  6. To serve, place the noodles on a large serving platter. Top with the meat and vegetables, then ladle some broth over the top.
  7. Garnish with fresh dill and serve hot.
